Semper ad Meliora

Data Scientist and Experimental Particle Physicist

Welcome to my corner on the web. Here I am storing my portfolio, projects, academic life, and some thoughts. I am an Ecuadorian scientist keen on Experimental Physics, Particle Detectors and Data Science, with more than 2 years of experience in institutions such as: Compact Muon Solenoid (CMS) at CERN and Universidad San Francisco de Quito.

My primary research area centers on the CMS one of the CERN-LHC experiments. Working at CERN has given me the opportunity to collaborate with large multicultural groups both, in-person and remotely. Throughout my career, I have gained experience in the analysis of datasets produced in high-energy collisions. I have conducted particle events analysis, reproducing the search for new physics events, processed large datasets and used advanced statistical tools.

In addition to my physics research, I have leveraged my programming proficiency in Python and C++ to develop and optimize data processing pipelines. Notably, I have employed Python for Extract, Transform, Load (ETL) processes in data analysis, integrating data from various sources, performing data cleaning, and loading it into target systems for comprehensive analysis. This experience has enhanced my ability to manage and analyze complex datasets effectively.

My contributions in particle detectors perfomance have been done through the use of programming languages like Python and C++. As a skilled communicator, I have presented my research to different audiences. My scientific curiosity and enthusiasm have inspired me to explore machine learning algorithms and particle detectors to optimize data processing.

I believe that bridging the knowledge between data science, nuclear and particle physics applications, could help us to move our society to a more sustainable way.
